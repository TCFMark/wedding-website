How we met

We met for a walk during lockdown in February 2021 after spending a week talking all sorts of nonsense on Tinder. The walk was well below freezing and Mark almost fell in the Physic Well outside Cumnor after stamping on some ice. Since then, we've made a home together mainly by putting three cats in a house and watching the carnage. Mark impulsively proposed on 15th July 2023 on Magdalen Bridge in Oxford after a long day out and then we had to wait over two more weeks for the ring to arrive.

Order of the day

Arrive 12.30-1pm

Arrive from 12.30pm and have a drink while we wait for the bride to arrive. There is a pub just around the corner in case you're early and need somewhere to pass the time! A bar will be available all day (though maybe don't get a round in during the ceremony).

Ceremony 1.30pm

Sarah and Mark will be tying the knot, jumping the broom and saying the vows in a short ceremony with help from our celebrant and maybe even the children in the room, followed by the usual walking around and photography.

"Breakfast" 3pm

We plan to provide some tasty yet casual food and wine (etc.) to cater for all. Please make sure to include your dietary requirements in your RSVP as we want to make sure everyone is looked after!

Speeches and toasts After that

As is traditional we will be doing some talking and also asking some of those close to us to talk a bit as well.

Friends, family, drinks and music Until late (11pm)

We invite everyone to stick around as little or as long as you like to celebrate our special day with us, eat cake, drink things and flail with some music on if that's your thing.

Hotels

Local hotels in the area. Witney and Eynsham are nearby, and Botley in Oxford is only 15 minutes by car if that's more convenient. There are plenty of other places to stay so do shop around if these aren't for you!

The Talbot Inn, Eynsham

A family-run inn just on our side of Eynsham (5 mins by car).

The Evenlode Hotel, Eynsham

Hotel and pub on the outskirts of Eynsham with easy access to the A40 (8 mins by car).

The Chequers Inn, Cassington

Welcoming local inn serving Indian food (9 mins by car).

Bear and Ragged Staff, Cumnor

The Bear and Ragged Staff is a historical inn just outside Oxford where they push the mayor of Cumnor into the pond for fun on a regular basis (12 mins by car).

The Blue Boar, Witney

A former coaching inn on Witney's market square (13 mins by car).

The Rose Revived, Newbridge

Chef & Brewer Inn with riverside views and walks (10 mins by car).

The Fleece, Witney

An inn and renowned gastropub on Witney's Church Green (12 mins by car).

The Bird in Hand, Witney

Classic Cotswolds country inn just outside the small village Hailey (18 mins by car).

Old Swan, Minster Lovell

Country inn with bushes and flowers in the picturesque village of Minster Lovell (15 mins by car).

The Trout at Tadpole Bridge, Bampton

Beautiful riverside pub with excellent food that we don't visit often enough (18 mins by car).

Fleur de Lys, Bampton

Warm and welcoming bistro with French fare near the centre of Bampton (17 mins by car).

Premier Inn, Botley, Oxford

For a reliable and inexpensive night or two, the Premier Inn in Botley is a good bet, just off the A34 (13 mins by car).

Premier Inn, Witney

For a reliable and inexpensive night or two, the Premier Inn in Witney is slightly closer and just off the A40 (11 mins by car).

Travelodge, Witney

Our nearest budget-friendly chain hotel with easy access to the A40 (9 mins by car).
